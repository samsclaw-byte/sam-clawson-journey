Before EVERY Hard Workout: Calf Protocol (5-7 minutes)

Sam, your calves are your Achilles heel (literally). This isn't optional.

Phase 1: Warm-Up (2 min)

  • 1 min: March in place ‚Üí gentle jog on spot
  • 1 min: Ankle circles (10 each direction, each foot)

Phase 2: Activation (3 min)

  • 20 x Calf raises (slow, full range) ‚Äî hold top 2 seconds
  • 20 x Single-leg balance touches (light, controlled)
  • 30 sec: Downward dog ‚Üí pedal feet (stretch calves dynamically)
  • 30 sec: World's greatest stretch (each side)

Phase 3: Test (30 sec)

  • 10 gentle skips on spot
  • STOP if: Sharp pain, tightness that doesn't ease, or "that feeling"

üí™ TRAINER'S CORNER

"Consistency > Intensity. Better to do 70% effort and come back tomorrow than 100% and get injured."

Key Principles

1. The 70% Rule

Always leave energy in the tank. If you feel like you could do 3 more reps, stop. The goal is to train again tomorrow, not to destroy yourself today.

2. Calf Awareness

Your calves are your limiting factor. Respect them. If they feel tight, switch to upper body or active recovery. No exceptions.

3. Sleep First

With a newborn and 5.5-6 hours sleep, recovery is compromised. Prioritize sleep over workouts when needed. One missed workout won't hurt; chronic sleep deprivation will.

4. Friday Afternoons

This is your window for longer sessions. Use it! 60-minute workouts, swimming, or just catching up on missed sessions from the week.
